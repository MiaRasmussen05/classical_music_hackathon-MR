Lesson 1: The Musical Alphabet

There are 8 white notes. We name notes from A to G and then repeat from A again. The black keys have names too. They are named as either sharp (#) or flat (b) of the note beside them. For example, the black note right of F is F#, and it is also left of G, so it can be called Gb too. This means that there are two possible names for all the black notes.

These 12 notes just repeat over and over again on the keyboard. Try studying the notes below and practice playing them on the piano.

Notes Keyboard Position
C This is the white on the left of the group of the two black keys
C♯ or D♭ This is the black note inbetween C and D
D This is the white in the middle of the group of the two black keys
D# or Eb This is the Black note inbetween D and E
E This is the white on the right of the group of the two black keys
F This is the white on the left of the group of the three black keys
F# or Gb This is the black note inbetween F and G
G This is the white note is in the middle left of the group of the three black keys
G# or Ab This is the black note inbetween G and A
A This is the white note in the middle right of the group of the three black keys

Lesson 2: Scales

Scales are often a piano student’s first step in learning how to play to the piano. While practicing scales can seem to be a tedious, routine exercise, scales provide the fundamentals for understanding music and mastering piano techniques.In general, it's a good idea to start with the major scales first, then move on to the natural minor, harmonic minor, and melodic minor scales. Once you master those you can move onto things like chromatic scales, ​blues scales, pentatonic scales and whole tone scales.

Major Scale

Below we have a scale in C major. All major scales are layed out the same way. We have a breaksown of a tone followed by a tone then followed by a semitone then 3 more tones and a semTone and finally a tone. Major scales are made up of intervals in this order tone tone semitone, tone tone tone, seimtone. You may be asking whatis a tone and what is a semi tone. To picture what this means on a keyboard everytime you step up a note you step a semi tone. E.G. C - C# is a semitone likewise E - F is a semitone. A tone or whole tone as it is commonly reffered to is a step of two semitones. E.G of this is A - B or F# - G#. With this system you can play a major scale starting on any note. As you played through some scales di you notice anything about the sound of the scales. Each scale has its own characteristic. As you can tell the sequence of intevals being whole tones and semi tones is the same for all major scales. So no matter where you start it all major scales sound very similar to eachother. Try and keep this sound in mind as you move onto the next section about minor scales and well compare the two to see if you notice the difference or even better can hear the difference.

Minor Scale

Below we have a scale in C harmonic minor. There is actually 3 minor scales. We will just focus on the harmonic minor for now and we will get into the details of the minor key in later lessons. Harmonic minor scales are made up of intervals in this order tone semitone, tone tone semitone, tone and a helf, seimtone. If you guessed that tone and a half meant three semitones then yes you would be correct. An example of thiws would be C - Eb or A - C. Click the play button and try this out for yourself. Lesson 3: Chords

Chord are made up of two or more Intervals. An interval is the distance between two notes. Building of of the last lesson on scales we can simply look at an interval as how many semitones are there inbetween the two notes. There is better and more practical ways of describing these intervals just like the minor third we explained earlier however we will need to lay the foundation of kys and intervals in this mnner in later lesson.
