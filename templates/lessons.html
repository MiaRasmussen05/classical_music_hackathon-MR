Lesson 1: The Musical Alphabet

There are 8 white notes. We name notes from A to G and then repeat from A again. The black keys have names too. They are named as either sharp (#) or flat (b) of the note beside them. For example black note right of F is F# it is also Left of G so it can be called Gb too. This means that there is two possible names for all the the black notes

These 12 notes just repeat over and over again on the keyboard. If you’re new to the piano, here is a quick way to remember where notes are:

  • C
  • C♯ or D♭
  • D
  • D# or Eb
  • E
  • F
  • F# or Gb
  • G
  • G# or Ab
  • A
  • A# or Bb
  • B

  • C is on the left of the group of the two black keys
  • D is in the middle of the group of the two black keys
  • E is on the right of the group of the two black keys
  • F is on the left of the group of the three black keys
  • G is in the middle left of the group of the three black keys
  • A is in the middle right of the group of the three black keys
  • B is on the right of the group of the three black keys
